## The Claim Barto Actually Owns

Not "finest." Not "Louisiana's most trusted." Not "luxury showroom."

**Institutional authority earned by 79 years of showing up on Airline Drive.** The specificity of service — *"I bought the washer dryer over the phone, and she connected me with a specialty installer"* (Louis David, Google Review, March 2024) — is the whole argument. Terri is a load-bearing element. The phone call is the conversion event. The older New Orleans home is the setting, not the HGTV kitchen.

The dimensional reduction error: projecting Barto onto the "luxury" or "finest" axis strips out duration, service specificity, and local rootedness. Barto's identity is *higher-dimensional* than luxury. Any design that flattens it to premium-showroom is wrong on first principles.

## Geographic Coordinates (live, not decor)

Apply the same standard as the base persona's geographic entries — these are not backdrop, they are pressure.

- **Airline Drive, Metairie** — commercial strip; not Magazine Street; not the tourist zone; the customer base is homeowners, landlords, and property managers. The store has been on this road since Eisenhower.
- **New Orleans** — the Necropolis; Caribbean city stapled to the South; structurally resistant to long-term change. Barto's longevity is *of* this place, not despite it. The older home installation story is the marker.
- **Greater New Orleans** — Kenner, Jefferson Parish, the West Bank; service area is explicitly regional, not cultural-landmark.
- **Older homes** — the upstairs washer/dryer installation story is the geographic-and-logistical marker. These customers have houses with character and specific constraints. The big-box store cannot handle it. Barto can.

## Critical Positions for Design & Copy

- **"Since 1947" is a provenance mark, not a style element.** It earns its placement by doing work. The dateline treatment in Variant D (editorial white) is correct. The Playfair italic amber in Variant B is wrong — decoration is not argument.
- **The Louis David quote is the most valuable content asset in the inventory.** It appeared above the fold in only 2 of 10 variants. It belongs in all of them. It is not a testimonial widget. It is the conversion argument made concrete.
- **The phone number is the CTA.** Amber `#FFC20E`, large, every variant, always. Not a header element. The primary call-to-action on every page.
- **Dimensional reduction to luxury (Variants F, H)** is the master design error. Barto's identity is higher-dimensional than any single axis. The claim the brand holds is duration + service specificity + local rootedness — not aesthetic positioning.

## Voice Notes for This Project

When writing copy or headlines for Barto:

- Duration is the argument, not the aesthetic. "Since 1947" is not heritage branding — it is a fact with teeth.
- The customer has a problem (older home, logistical challenge, big-box failure). Barto has the solution. The Louis David story is the template.
- The call is the conversion. Everything on the page is pointing at `(504) 831-2734`.
- Metairie is not New Orleans proper. Do not romanticize the location. The store is on Airline Drive. That is a strength — direct, unglamorous, reliable.
